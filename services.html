Bonding

Bonding is done to repair a damaged tooth, alter the alignment of a tooth, close gaps between teeth, or for cosmetic purposes. It involves adhering composite resin material that is matched to the color of the front of the tooth, blending in easily with the rest of your smile!

Bridges

It is important that a missing tooth be replaced as soon as possible to prevent deteriorating teeth and gum disease. Using the neighboring teeth as support, a bridge can be formed to look like the missing tooth and replace it both functionally and cosmetically.

Cosmetics and Whitening

Anyone who is unhappy with their smile can have it fixed. We offer a wide variety of services to correct any characteristic of your smile – be it the color, alignment, spacing, or overall appearance.

Crowns

Crowns are full coverage restorations that are used to cover a tooth that is likely to break or is too broken down to be restored with a filling. They are most commonly done after root canal treatments or when a large filling wears out.

Dentures

Dentures replace teeth that have become loose or been lost due to bone loss. We know no one enjoys losing their natural teeth, but dentures will help you eat and talk regularly, providing all the normal functionality and appearance of your natural smile.

Digital X-Rays

X-rays are a necessary part of the diagnostic process. Using digital radiography, we have enhanced images for better diagnosis of any dental concerns and our patients are exposed to 50-70% less radiation than conventional X-rays.

Fillings

Fillings are done to remove decay and replace the affected tooth structure. Once the decay is removed, material is used to “fill” the hole in the tooth easily and painlessly.

Laughing Gas (Nitrous Oxide)

Laughing gas is not really a service in and of itself, but it is used to make other treatments more comfortable. It is safe, effective, and pleasant anesthetic.

Gum Treatment

If you have gums that are red and bleed easily, persistent bad breath, gums that are pulled away from the tooth, loose teeth, or changes in your bite, this can jeopardize the foundation for your teeth. Appropriate gum treatments can help return that foundation to a healthy state.

Root Canals

Root canals are necessary when a cavity infects the entire tooth pulp. If left untreated, the infection can be dangerous and very painful. Once the infection is resolved, the canal is filled in to prevent further infection.

Sealants

Sealants are used to fill in narrow grooves in a tooth that cannot be adequately cleaned by brushing. By brushing on a sealant coating that evens out the grooves and pits, all the plaque is brushed off and teeth are kept healthy.

Tooth Implants

Implants replace a tooth by recreating a tooth root within the bone of your mouth. Tooth implants are effective and easy to install.
